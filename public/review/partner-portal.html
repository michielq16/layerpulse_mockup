The one-sentence version

Two homes, not one

A partner manages 10–50 customer tenants. Until now the mockup only had the customer view — one tenant at a time. The partner needs a level above that: a portal showing the whole portfolio, with a clear way to dive into any one customer.

So there are now two environments. The partner portal (the book-wide view) and the customer environment (the single-tenant view we already built). You move between them with one click.

How it's shaped

The partner pages

Nine surfaces — three are brand new

Six are the standard partner toolkit. The three marked New don't exist anywhere in the current product — they're the partner's reasons to log in daily.

Overview

The €-ranked "fix first" queue across every customer — what to action today, and for whom.

Customers

A triage table, worst-health first: health, spend, wasted €, top issue, one-click "Act as".

Connections

The invitation lifecycle — who's connected, what's pending, what to re-invite.

Activity

One feed of invitations, access changes, capacity events and billing across the book.

QBR Builder New

Pick a customer, toggle the evidence, and get a ready-to-send quarterly-review one-pager. The partner's value story, in seconds.

Benchmarks New

Rank every customer on one measure (health, waste, doc coverage…). Instantly see the outliers worth a call.

Team & Seats New

Who on the partner team watches which customer — and which customers have no owner (a coverage gap).

Billing

The partner's own LayerPulse subscription — plan, seats, quota, invoices.

Settings

Partner profile, defaults, and notification preferences.
